Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue.

  • Certain columns, like ‘cast’ and ‘genres’, contain multiple values separated by pipe (|) characters.
  • The final two columns ending with “_adj” show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  1. Which genres are most popular from year to year?
  2. What kinds of properties are associated with movies that have high revenues?
  3. Which genre has, on average, the lowest movie popularity score?
  4. What is the revenue generated yearly?
  5. Which movie made the max profit?
  6. Which movie made the worse loss?
  7. What's the profit trends of movies over the years
  8. What's movie genre has the highest mean runtime?
  9. What is the trend on movie popularity?
  10. What is the relationship between budget, revenue and profit distribution?

Conclusions¶

The following can be concluded from the analysis that:

  • The number of movies produced at every 20 years interval from 1960 always triples.
  • The average budget for movies is gradually increasing
  • The average revenue generated from movies is gradually increasing
  • The average profit made on movies is gradually increasing

    This could be as a result increment in world's population because world population also increases with time. This could also be as a result of improvement in mass media technology and easy access to movies as time runs by.

    Limitations

    During data cleaning, I replaced missing budget and revenue values with the mean value for that per. This is an assumption. For further research, a machine learning algorithm can be used to predict this missing values. This would further increase the accuracy of our analysis.

    I also was unable to split the genre column and give more insights on genre data. I also was unable to check details of the production_companies details.
